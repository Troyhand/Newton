# Newton Glossary

## Newton
A governed cognitive substrate for next-generation Personal Assistant systems.

## Personal Assistant Layer
The user-facing interaction layer where humans communicate with AI systems.

## Governed Cognitive Substrate
The structured reasoning and validation layer beneath the assistant interface.

## c-Core
Newton’s deterministic foundation for stability, auditability, drift resistance, and governed cognitive workflows.

## Domain Pack
A structured knowledge module used to support domain-bounded reasoning and validation.

## Session Share
A trusted synchronization framework for sharing reasoning context, workflow state, and continuity between authorized Newton environments.

## Drift Resistance
The ability of a system to preserve reasoning stability and avoid uncontrolled behavioral or interpretive changes over time.

## Provenance Tracking
The process of recording where information came from, how it was used, and whether it can be verified.

## Auditability
The ability to inspect, verify, and trace system actions, reasoning paths, and governance events.

## Validation-First Workflow
A workflow where claims, actions, or outputs are tested against rules, evidence, constraints, or trusted domain structures before being accepted.

## Human Concurrence
A structured approval model where human authority remains involved in high-trust governance or validation decisions.

## GCIP
Governed Cognitive Infrastructure Protocol.

GCIP is Newton’s protocol-level framework for governed cognition, signed trust, deterministic validation, domain-bounded reasoning, Session Share compatibility, and drift-resistant Personal Assistant infrastructure.
